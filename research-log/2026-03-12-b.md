# 2026-03-12 — Size dependence at L=256: equilibration problem

## What was run

Fractional sweep at L=256 (256×256, rm=4, max_degree=8, 8 seeds) across
prob=0.33–0.50. First attempt used 10k frames; second targeted rerun used
30k frames at prob=0.33 and prob=0.35 (4 seeds each).

## Results

**10k frames, L=256 (all probabilities):**

Every seed at every probability returned bd≈0.50, deg≈4.0, var≈2e-5. The
system had not moved from its random initial state. Degree stuck at ~4.0
(initial degree) indicates essentially no topology evolution occurred.

**30k frames, L=256 (prob=0.33 and prob=0.35 only):**

| prob | seed | bd     | var    | deg  |
|------|------|--------|--------|------|
| 0.33 | 0    | 0.3437 | 0.0075 | 4.26 |
| 0.33 | 1    | 0.3333 | 0.0073 | 4.30 |
| 0.33 | 2    | 0.3539 | 0.0090 | 4.24 |
| 0.33 | 3    | 0.3235 | 0.0062 | 4.31 |
| 0.35 | 0    | 0.5329 | 0.0000 | 3.98 |
| 0.35 | 1    | 0.5330 | 0.0000 | 3.98 |
| 0.35 | 2    | 0.5327 | 0.0000 | 3.98 |
| 0.35 | 3    | 0.5335 | 0.0000 | 4.00 |

## Interpretation

**p=0.33 at 30k frames is still mid-transition.** bd moved from ≈0.50 (10k
frames) to ≈0.33 (30k frames) and degree is climbing from 4.0 to 4.25. The
system is ordering but has not equilibrated. At L=128, p=0.33 reaches
bd≈0.10 and deg≈7.39 within 30k frames. At L=256 it needs considerably
longer.

**p=0.35 at 30k frames is solidly disordered and equilibrated.** bd≈0.53,
deg≈3.98, near-zero variance — this is the same state seen at p=0.37 and
above at L=128.

**The 10k frame run was simply insufficient.** Degree frozen at 4.0 after
10k frames is a transient artefact — the topology co-evolution has not had
enough time to act. The result should not be interpreted as absence of an
ordered phase.

## Equilibration time scaling

At L=128, ordering completes within ~5–10k frames (degree reaches 7+ quickly
from random init at p=0.33). At L=256, 30k frames leaves the system at
deg≈4.25 — less than 10% of the way to the expected equilibrium degree.
This suggests ordering time scales faster than linearly with N (number of
nodes). A rough estimate: L=256 needs ≥120k frames to equilibrate.

The reason is not fully understood. The topo co-evolution rate per frame
(~4 events/frame) is independent of N, but the number of edges that need
to restructure to reach the ordered phase scales with N. This gives an
ordering time that scales as O(N) in frames = O(L²) in frames.

## What this does and does not tell us

**Does not tell us:** Whether the first-order transition and bimodality persist
at L=256. The run was too short to reach equilibrium in the ordered phase.

**Does tell us:** The transition at L=256 has shifted — p=0.35 is cleanly
disordered and equilibrated after 30k frames, whereas at L=128, p=0.35 was
the coexistence point with bimodal seed behaviour. This could indicate the
critical point shifts to lower mutation probability at larger L, or it could
reflect that p=0.35 at L=256 is genuinely disordered while p=0.33 (still
ordering) is near the new critical point.

## Immediate next steps

1. **Ordered-init test at L=256** — run p=0.33 and p=0.35 with
   `--ordered-init`. Fast to run. If the ordered phase is stable at p=0.33
   but not p=0.35, that pins the critical point. If ordered-init also decays
   at p=0.33, the ordered phase does not exist at L=256 regardless of runtime.

2. **Long run at L=256, p=0.33** — run to 120k frames with random init to
   confirm equilibration to bd≈0.10 and deg≈7+. Confirms timescale hypothesis.
   Needed before any L-dependent size scaling analysis can be trusted.
